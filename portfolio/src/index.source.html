01 // Profile

About Me

I am a Medical Laboratory Science student with interests in cardiovascular health, genetics, biomedical research, and healthcare innovation. My academic journey is focused on understanding disease mechanisms through laboratory science while building the foundation for a future career in medicine.

My current research explores ACE I/D polymorphism and its relationship with cardiometabolic risk factors in a Ghanaian population. Through this experience, I am developing skills in molecular techniques, laboratory investigations, and data analysis while gaining exposure to biomedical research.

Beyond the laboratory, I am developing skills in data science and scientific communication. My long-term ambition is to become a cardiothoracic surgeon who combines clinical expertise, research, and innovation to improve patient care.

Final Year Research Project

ACE I/D Polymorphism and Cardiometabolic Risk Factors in a Ghanaian Population

TECHNIQUE_01 PCR Amplification
TECHNIQUE_02 DNA Extraction
TECHNIQUE_03 Genotyping
TECHNIQUE_04 Data Analysis

My current final year research project investigates how the ACE insertion/deletion (I/D) polymorphism relates to cardiometabolic risk factors — including hypertension and metabolic syndrome — within a Ghanaian population, with the aim of informing early risk stratification.

Section_01 · In_Preparation

Research Background

Literature overview of the ACE I/D polymorphism, the renin–angiotensin system, and reported links to hypertension and cardiometabolic disease. Documentation to be added as the project progresses.
