About Me

I am a Medical Laboratory Sciences student interested in cardiovascular health, genetics, and biomedical innovation. My academic work focuses on disease mechanisms through laboratory science. This prepares me for a future career in medicine.

My current research focuses on the ACE I/D polymorphism and its effect on cardiometabolic risk in a Ghanaian population. This work is sharpening my skills in molecular techniques and biomedical data analysis.

Outside the lab, I am strengthening my abilities in data science and scientific communication. My ultimate aim is to become a cardiothoracic surgeon who combines clinical practice, research, and innovation to enhance patient outcomes.

Final Year Research Project

ACE I/D Polymorphism and Cardiometabolic Risk Factors in a Ghanaian Population

TECHNIQUE 01 PCR Amplification
TECHNIQUE 02 DNA Extraction
TECHNIQUE 03 Genotyping
TECHNIQUE 04 Data Analysis

My current final year research project investigates how the ACE insertion/deletion (I/D) polymorphism relates to cardiometabolic risk factors, including hypertension and metabolic syndrome within a Ghanaian population, with the aim of informing early risk stratification.

Research Question

Does the ACE insertion/deletion (I/D) polymorphism influence cardiometabolic risk factors among hypertensive and normotensive Ghanaian adults?

Study Population

Ghanaian adults; both hypertensive and normotensive participants, recruited from the Tamale Teaching Hospital.

Methods
  • DNA extraction
  • PCR amplification
  • Agarose gel electrophoresis
  • Genotyping
  • Statistical analysis (SPSS)
